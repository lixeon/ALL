# Manuscript Draft v1 (Demo)

## Title
Academic Lobster Lab: A Hierarchical OpenClaw Multi-Agent Research Team with Reviewer-Driven Iteration and Reproducibility Governance

## Abstract
We present a hierarchical 9-agent academic research team configuration for OpenClaw. The system explicitly separates leadership, execution, critique, and reproducibility governance. We introduce a workflow that forces reject→revision→resubmit iterations under peer-review simulation, and a literature knowledge base that preserves original PDFs with OCR outputs and provenance records. This draft is a demonstration artifact; claims are placeholders pending empirical validation.

## 1. Introduction
Modern LLM agent systems often optimize for task completion but under-specify reviewer-facing rigor and reproducibility. We argue that academic research workflows require explicit governance: leadership-level idea screening, senior-led execution, junior-led artifact production, external reviewer critique, and an independent reproducibility chair.

## 3. Method Overview
We define roles, delegation rules, and release gates. Reviewers may issue REJECT/MAJOR/MINOR/ACCEPT decisions; releases are blocked unless traceability passes.
