# End-to-End Demo Scenario

## Scenario
Build and harden a research idea for an OpenClaw-based academic multi-agent system until it survives elite peer review and reproducibility audit.

## Step 1: Idea Origination
- `academy-lead` proposes that scientific multi-agent systems should be evaluated not only on task completion but also on reviewer-facing rigor and reproducibility.

## Step 2: Program Definition
- `distinguished-scholar` converts the idea into a research program with core questions, contribution boundaries, and major workstreams.

## Step 3: Senior Workstream Split
- `senior-researcher-1` leads method and system protocol design.
- `senior-researcher-2` leads evaluation, baselines, and validity planning.

## Step 4: Junior Execution
- `junior-researcher-1` prepares experiment runs, tables, and structured outputs.
- `junior-researcher-2` reproduces reference baselines, organizes data, and prepares support artifacts.

## Step 5: Internal Synthesis
- `distinguished-scholar` integrates both senior tracks into a coherent thesis package.

## Step 6: External Discussion
- `peer-reviewer-1` attacks novelty and differentiation.
- `peer-reviewer-2` attacks evaluation and reproducibility.

## Step 7: Revision
- seniors revise weak points
- distinguished scholar updates the thesis package
- academy lead decides whether the idea is becoming significant enough

## Step 8: Reproducibility Audit
- `reproducibility-chair` checks artifacts, trace links, configs, and rerun readiness.

## Step 9: Release Decision
Release only if critique has materially strengthened the work and reproducibility passes.
